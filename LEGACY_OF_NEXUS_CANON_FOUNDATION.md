# Legacy of Nexus Design Foundation

## Core Identity
`Legacy of Nexus` is a hopeful sci-fi Buddy RPG where digital creatures spill into the real world during catastrophic glitch events. The player bonds with a permanent starter Buddy, builds a team of collectible companions, and helps shape a world learning to coexist with emotional digital lifeforms.

This game should feel original, character-driven, and stylish rather than like a gym-based monster RPG clone.

## Key Pillars
- `Dino Buddy` is the permanent mascot and emotional heart of the game.
- Single-player is the main experience, with optional drop-in co-op for up to `4` players.
- Exploration is built as a `3D` third-person adventure in `Godot`.
- Every Buddy starts with a neutral personality state and develops based on how the player talks to them.
- Capturing uses a rhythm and timing-based phone mechanic instead of throwable items.
- Story progression is driven by major `Crisis` events instead of gyms or a league.
- Fun comes from bonding, collecting, team-building, exploration, and stylish `2v2` battles.

## Starter Buddy Structure
The player chooses one permanent starter Buddy during the opening `Static Fall` event. That starter remains the player's anchor companion for the full game and cannot be removed from the active roster.

Rules:
- `Dino Buddy` is the mascot regardless of which starter the player chooses.
- The chosen starter gets the most dialogue, story reactions, and emotional weight.
- Other starter options can exist, but Dino Buddy remains the face of the world, brand, and sequel setup.
- The starter bond is meant to feel personal and story-defining, not interchangeable.

## Personality System
Every Buddy begins at a neutral emotional baseline. Their long-term personality develops through player interaction, especially dialogue choices and how the player treats them in and out of battle.

Core design rules:
- Buddies react to tone, trust, and consistency.
- Dialogue should shape behavior over time, not just produce one-off approval meters.
- Personality growth affects battle lines, side scenes, passive bonuses, and synergy behavior.
- A player who is supportive, reckless, demanding, or compassionate should gradually shape different Buddy responses.

This system makes the collection loop more personal, because two players can capture the same Buddy but end up with different relationships.

## Story Spine

### Opening: Static Fall
A massive glitch event called the `Static Fall` tears open the sky over a city park. Digital Buddies spill into reality, injured and terrified. People panic, opportunists move in, and the player finds one wounded Buddy trying to survive the chaos. That Buddy touches the player's phone, digitizes into it, and forms the first bond.

This opening supports both solo and multiplayer:
- Solo: the player begins their personal journey with their first Buddy.
- Co-op: each player can have their own first-bond story, then join up later.

### Act 1
The player learns how to live with Buddies while dealing with unstable glitch zones, frightened new creatures, and people trying to exploit them. The early game is about discovery, trust, and understanding what Buddies are.

### Act 2
Glitches spread into new regions. Human society starts splitting into different camps and philosophies around Buddies: friendship, profit, control, freedom, and fear. The player begins to understand that this is not just a local accident.

### Act 3
The player discovers the origin of the Buddies: an old `Nexus` lab experiment that gave digital life real emotional awareness. The final conflict is not about defeating a league or proving strength. It is about choosing the future relationship between humans and Buddies.

## The Five Legendaries
Each legendary is tied to a major theme and a major story arc.

### Aether
- Theme: memory
- Role: reveals the buried past of the glitch events and the earliest emotional Buddies
- Story function: shows that Buddies carry memory, loss, and inherited identity

### Nexus
- Theme: control
- Role: primary antagonist force
- Story function: represents the original system that tried to contain, shape, and own digital life

### Vortex
- Theme: freedom
- Role: challenges human assumptions about ownership and consent
- Story function: forces the player to distinguish between bonding and control

### Eclipse
- Theme: identity
- Role: confronts the instability of created life that was edited, discarded, or altered
- Story function: asks what it means for a created being to define itself

### Helix
- Theme: evolution
- Role: mythic future-facing legendary
- Story function: points toward what Buddies can become beyond their original design

## Endings

### Bittersweet Ending
The player sacrifices `Dino Buddy` to seal the rift forever. The world is saved, but the emotional cost defines the ending. This path should hurt, feel meaningful, and close the story on loss.

### True / Canon Ending
The player defeats `Nexus` without sacrificing Dino Buddy. Good Buddies remain in the real world, coexistence becomes possible, and the future opens instead of closing. This ending clearly sets up `Legacy of Nexus 2`, with surviving Nexus scientists having digitized themselves and preparing to return.

## World Structure
The world is fully open and seamless, built around three major regions instead of a long chain of small routes.

Presentation rule:
- The player explores the world in third-person `3D`, with room for traversal, camera-driven discovery, and cinematic Buddy interactions.

### Neon Sprawl
- Role: main urban hub and social center
- Tone: dense, vibrant, stylish, commercially alive, full of screens and shifting public opinion
- Function: the player's main city anchor, early systems hub, and central social space

### Glitchwood
- Role: wild region overtaken by unstable digital growth
- Tone: eerie, beautiful, overgrown, and unpredictable
- Function: a place of strange encounters, Buddy mysteries, and spreading instability

### Shattered Coast
- Role: late-game frontier where reality is visibly breaking down
- Tone: stormy, dramatic, fractured, and dangerous
- Function: escalation zone for major story reveals, Crisis events, and legendary encounters

### Regional Hub Rule
Each major region should include its own smaller hub area where the player can heal, prepare, trade, take side quests, and reconnect with NPCs.

## Crisis Structure
Progression is driven by `5` major Crisis events rather than gyms.

Design rules:
- Each Crisis is a large story milestone with social and world impact.
- Resolving a Crisis changes public perception, unlocks new areas, and escalates the mystery.
- Crisis events should feel like the player is helping stabilize a changing world, not simply earning badges.

Recommended escalation:
1. `Static Fall` introduces the first bond and the new reality.
2. A major urban instability Crisis reshapes `Neon Sprawl`.
3. A spreading anomaly Crisis deepens the mystery in `Glitchwood`.
4. A rupture Crisis in `Shattered Coast` pushes the world to the edge.
5. The final `Nexus` Crisis decides the future of humans and Buddies.

## Battle Identity
Battles are turn-based, readable, and expressive.

Core rules:
- `2v2` pair battles are the foundation in both solo and co-op play.
- Battles transition out of exploration into a dedicated battle arena scene for stronger presentation and cleaner combat readability.
- Personality influences move behavior, reactions, and synergy moments.
- Bond level changes dialogue, combo potential, and how Buddies respond under pressure.
- Team building should reward pair chemistry, not just raw stats.

The goal is to make every battle feel like a relationship in motion, not just a number check.

## Capture And Bonding
Capture should feel personal, skill-based, and fictional to the setting.

### Capture Flow
1. Find or encounter a wild or unstable Buddy.
2. Weaken, calm, or stabilize it.
3. Use the phone to match its digital rhythm.
4. Complete the sync and digitize the Buddy into the player's device.

### Bonding Rules
- Higher bond unlocks new dialogue, passive bonuses, and combo moves.
- The way the player speaks to a Buddy affects personality growth over time.
- Bonding should influence both story scenes and battle performance.
- Capturing a Buddy is only the beginning; the relationship is the real progression system.

## Multiplayer Direction
Multiplayer is optional and should always feel like an extension of the single-player world.

Rules:
- Up to `4` players can drop into shared sessions.
- Solo players can experience the full story without missing critical content.
- Co-op supports exploration, battles, trading, and shared Buddy hunting.
- Multiplayer should increase fun and social discovery, not become mandatory for progression.

## Engine Direction
`Legacy of Nexus` is being built in `Godot` with a `3D` production target.

Current implementation target:
- third-person overworld exploration
- separate battle scene transitions
- prototype-first workflow using placeholder geometry before character art and Buddy models are ready

## Canon Summary
`Legacy of Nexus` is about bonding with digital life, navigating a world in transition, and deciding whether coexistence is built through empathy or control. Dino Buddy is the mascot, the emotional centerpiece, and the clearest symbol of what is at stake.
